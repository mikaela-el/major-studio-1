How Does Electrification in Rural Africa Affect Years of Schooling?
When a rural household does not have access to electricity, they must rely on traditional fuel sources for domestic activities. According to the FAO, women bear the largest share of time and labor burdens associated with collecting fuel for domestic use. Although there are several factors that affect female years of schooling, household electrification has the ability to relieve time and labor burdens for rural women, allowing them to reallocate their time towards schooling.
